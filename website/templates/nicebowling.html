Gameplay

Nice Bowling is an unfair bowling game that was inspired by Wii Sports and WarioWare.

Randomly selected effects are applied to the ball, pins, lane, and/or environment at the start of each frame- each bowling frome, not each time the GPU redraws the screen. Even I think that would be way too much.

The game features pass and play multiplayer- the worst kind of multiplayer.

There's a high score system. I thought it would add to the absurdity since your score will mostly be a product of dumb luck.

The soft-body bowling ball is my favorite effect.

About

This was the first video game I ever made and I learned a lot during its development. I ended up releasing it on Google Play and Amazon but Google started requiring hefty privacy policies to be included into the game itself and that kind of turned out to be a nail in the coffin as it was already on a version of Unity3D that Google Play didn't support. So I said no thanks. Originally, I planned to make a PC release but really, really hated the controls. They were too complicated when I tried to add spin to the ball based on how the player swipes their finger. That one single movement decided the speed, angle, and spin and it became really frustrating for players. Which, admittedly, was kinda funny. In my attempts to address player complaints feedback, I added even more math to make the swipes straighter to fairly disappointing results. After I abandoned Nice Bowling, my friend’s 11-year-old son was restless in a restaurant and his dad suggested playing 'the bowling game' which he instantly remembered me foisting it on him to playtest and promptly started crying. Lol git gud, kid.

Since I'm not working on this project and have not worked on it in several years, I'm opening the game's source code and inviting any fools on the internet who dare try and make a better version to knock themselves out. We need more games that make kids cry. If you do something with my game, let me know and I'll play it. I'll also link it here.
